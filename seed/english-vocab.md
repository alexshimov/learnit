en: to postpone
ru: отложить, перенести (на более поздний срок)
usage: to delay an event to a later time; neutral or formal register.
example: They **postponed** the meeting until Friday.
example: The launch was **postponed** because of the storm.
antonyms: to bring forward, to advance
related: to delay, to defer, to reschedule, to put off

---

en: reluctant
ru: неохотный; делающий что-то без желания
usage: unwilling and hesitant to do something.
example: She was **reluctant** to admit her mistake.
example: He gave a **reluctant** nod.
antonyms: eager, willing, keen
related: hesitant, unwilling, averse, loath

---

en: thorough
ru: тщательный, основательный
usage: complete, with attention to every detail.
example: They did a **thorough** review of the code.
example: a **thorough** cleaning of the flat
antonyms: superficial, careless, sloppy
related: meticulous, rigorous, careful, exhaustive

---

en: meticulous
ru: дотошный, скрупулёзный
usage: showing great and precise attention to detail.
example: a **meticulous** planner
example: She is **meticulous** about her notes.
antonyms: careless, slapdash
related: thorough, precise, diligent, painstaking
