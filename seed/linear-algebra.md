Q: What is an eigenvector of a matrix A?
A: A non-zero vector **v** whose direction is unchanged by A — it is only scaled: `A·v = λv`.

---

Q: What is the scalar λ by which an eigenvector is stretched or shrunk?
A: The **eigenvalue**.

---

Q: What does the determinant of a 2×2 matrix measure geometrically?
A: The signed area scaling factor of the linear map it represents.
reverse: true

---

Q: When is a square matrix invertible?
A: Exactly when its determinant is non-zero — equivalently, when its columns are linearly independent.

---

Q: What is the dimension of the space spanned by a matrix's columns called?
A: The **rank**.
hint: think "how many independent directions"
