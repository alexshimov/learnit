Q: What must a consequence connect to in order to motivate someone?
A: A **live interest** — something the person already values (their reputation, their craft, not letting teammates down).

::cascade
A live interest | what they value | find
A real consequence | already attached to it | then
Make it visible | motivation follows

::takeaway Does-it-land test
If they shrug, don't push the consequence harder — you mis-diagnosed the interest. Find another one.

---

Q: Why can't you simply "add motivation" to another person?
A: You can't manufacture motivation. You can only *reveal* a real consequence already attached to something they value.

::pull
You can't add motivation. You can only **reveal a consequence** already attached to a live interest.

---

Q: Name the three needs of Self-Determination Theory — the universal motivation levers the research backs.
A: **Autonomy**, **competence**, and **relatedness**.

::note SDT mapping
Independence → autonomy, achievement → competence, relationships → relatedness.

---

Q: A consequence often stays hidden because it happens "out of sight." Give one example for a developer.
A: Downstream breakage in another discipline, or bugs surfacing later in the player base — real effects happening where the developer never sees them.

::warn Common mistake
Surfacing a consequence the person can already see. If it were visible, it would already be motivating them.
